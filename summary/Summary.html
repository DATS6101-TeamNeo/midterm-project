1.Abstract

In this project, we aim to analyze the impact of major historical events on the S&P 500 index, focusing on the Great Recession, the COVID-19 pandemic, and the Russia-Ukraine conflict. By examining the indexs performance before and after these events, we seek to understand how economic downturns, geopolitical crises, and global pandemics influence stock market behavior.

The objective of this project is to explore the relationship between the S&P 500 index and these major historical events, providing insights into the indexs resilience, volatility, and performance during times of crisis. By analyzing the indexs behavior during these critical periods, we aim to uncover patterns, trends, and correlations useful for data-driven decision-making.

Our analysis will focus on key metrics such as volatility, returns, and performance indicators, comparing the S&P 500 indexs performance during the Great Recession, COVID-19 pandemic, and Russia-Ukraine conflict. By examining these three distinct periods, we aim to identify how the index responds to external shocks, recovers from downturns, and adapts to changing market conditions.

The results of this analysis can provide valuable insights for investors, financial analysts, and policymakers, helping them navigate turbulent market conditions, mitigate risks, and capitalize on opportunities. By understanding the impact of major historical events on the S&P 500 index, stakeholders can make more informed decisions, optimize their investment strategies, and achieve better outcomes in the stock market.

2. Introduction

The S&P 500 is a stock market index that measures the performance of 500 large-cap U.S. companies listed on stock exchanges. An index in finance refers to a statistical measure of change in a securities market. It typically represents a portfolio of securities that represent a particular market or sector. Stock market indices are used to track the performance of a specific group of stocks.overall, The index is market-capitalization -weighted, meaning that larger companies have a greater impact on its value. Now, coming to the S&P 500, it includes companies from diverse industries such as technology, healthcare, financial services, consumer discretionary and industries. The S&P 500 is widely regarded as one of the best benchmarks for the performance of the U.S. stock market.

This project utilizes a dataset from Yahoo finance containing information on over 6,000 instances, with the goal of developing Explanatory Data Analysis to effectively distinguish Data observations for analysis. The dataset includes characteristics such as open, close, High, Volume and Low. Through exploratory data analysis, we will identify trends and correlations between these features and gather insights. Firstly we will see if the dataset needs any changes like pre processing then we will continue with analysis using t-tests.

The results produced from this project can provide valuable insights into market dynamics, risk management, investment strategies, and decision-making, contributing to more informed and successful investing over time.

3. Dataset

Our dataset is available as S&P 500 (^GSPC) on Yahoo Finance website. All the data is sourced from Yahoo Finance website and covers data from 2003-2023. The dataset consists of 5285 data points (records) and 6 features(columns). Also, there are no null values in the dataset.

Features description is given below;

1. Open: Opening price of the S&P 500 index on a given date.

2. Close: Closing price of the S&P 500 index on a given date.

3. High: The highest price attained by S&P 500 index on a given date.

4. Low: The lowest price observed for the S&P 500 index on a given date.

5. Volume: The total number of shares of all S&P 500 index components traded on a given date.

6. Daily Change: The difference in price between the current days closing price and the previous days closing price.

SMART QUESTION : 1 How is the S&P500 Index affected by the Great Recession?

Explination: This question aims to explore the relationship between the S&P500 Index and the Great Recession, a significant economic event. We found that this event had a significant affect on S&P 500 index, so we formulated this question to further investigate this relationship further. (By analyzing the S&P500 Indexs performance during the Great Recession, we can gain insights into how the index was impacted by this economic downturn.)

Null Hypothesis : There is no significant difference between the S&P 500 Index before and after the Great Recession.

Alternate Hypothesis : There is a significant difference between the S&P 500 Index before and after the Great Recession.

## 
##  Welch Two Sample t-test
## 
## data:  new_before_gr$Close and new_after_gr$Close
## t = 4, df = 2514, p-value = 1e-04
## alternative hypothesis: true difference in means is not equal to 0
## 95 percent confidence interval:
##  13.3 41.4
## sample estimates:
## mean of x mean of y 
##      1218      1191

Observation:

We can conclude that there is strong evidence to reject the null hypothesis as the p values is less than the level of significance 0.05. The test indicates a statistically significant difference in the means of the S&P 500 index closing values before and after the specified periods. The results are as expected that is before GR the mean was higher compared to the mean after GR and the main reason behind it is the number of days market took for recovery that is 1403 days.The results (P-value and Means of groups) of the t-test provide statistical evidence to support this conclusion.

SMART QUESTION 2 : How is the S&P500 Index affected by the COVID-19 pandemic?

Covid-19 has several negative and positive effects on the Society and S&P500 as a whole

Positive Effects:

The COVID-19 outbreak acted as a catalyst, rapidly advancing the shift towards digitalization and remote operations across many sectors. This transition played into the strengths of major technology firms. Companies such as Amazon, Microsoft, Apple, and Google experienced a surge in the demand for their digital offerings and solutions that enabled remote work, online shopping, virtual communication, and digital entertainment. As a result of this heightened demand, the stocks of these tech giants saw substantial price increases. Given their significant weightings in the S&P 500 index, the stock price appreciation of these technology behemoths made a positive contribution to the overall performance of the benchmark index.

To mitigate the economic impacts of the lockdowns imposed due to the pandemic, central banks like the Federal Reserve and governments across the globe injected substantial amounts of capital and enacted stimulus programs. This massive influx of liquidity into the financial system helped stabilize and calm the markets during a period of heightened volatility and uncertainty. Moreover, these interventions bolstered investor sentiment and confidence in the markets resilience. Consequently, this renewed optimism among investors, fueled by the stimulus measures, had a favorable effect on the performance of the S&P 500 index. similarly these helped in growth of healthcare growth

Negative Effects:

Firstly, Industries such as travel, hospitality, and retail were severely affected by lockdowns and reduced consumer spending. Companies in these sectors experienced significant stock price declines, dragging down the overall performance of the S&P 500.

The pandemic caused extreme volatility in financial markets. Uncertainty about the viruss impact on the economy led to sharp market swings, causing fluctuations in the S&P 500 and increasing investor anxiety.

Null Hypothesis : There is no significant difference between the S&P 500 Index before and after the Covid-19.

Alternate Hypothesis : There is a significant difference between the S&P 500 Index before and after the Covid-19.

By comparing and performing analysis using t-test. we have found that the mean of close instance before covid and after covid is not as expected. the mean of close is higher after the covid by 1266 compared to before covid. Furthermore the p-value is less than 2e-16 which means we reject null hypothesis. After reading an article from Wall street journal we have come to know that covid effects were reduced and recovered within just 119 days which is very less compared to Great recession as the government got involved and helped the stock market recover faster than expected.
